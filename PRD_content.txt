1. Executive Summary
Job seeking today is a multi-channel, high-volume activity. A single seeker interacts with 200-500 companies across LinkedIn connection requests, HR cold-emails, InMail messages, career page applications, and follow-up reminders. The current workflow is fragmented: company shortlists live in spreadsheets, LinkedIn activity lives on LinkedIn, HR correspondence lives in Gmail, and follow-up dates live in the seeker's memory. This fragmentation causes missed follow-ups, duplicate outreach, lost context on which companies were already contacted, and an inability to measure funnel conversion (how many contacted companies actually replied or scheduled interviews).
This Product Requirements Document specifies a local-first, single-user web application that consolidates the entire job-seeking workflow into one dashboard. The application ingests a pre-curated company spreadsheet (the Kochi software companies xlsx with 302 rows and 19 columns including LinkedIn URLs, career pages, HR emails, and match scores), authenticates the user against LinkedIn using a hybrid approach (official OAuth2 for identity + Playwright-driven browser session for activity scraping), and continuously synchronizes LinkedIn events (connection requests sent, messages exchanged, profile views) back into the local database.
On top of this unified data layer, the application surfaces five operational capabilities: (1) job opening detection from both the company career page and the LinkedIn company jobs tab, (2) HR email tracking with one-click logging of emails sent, (3) follow-up scheduling with configurable reminder cadence, (4) LinkedIn activity tracking that auto-records every connection request and message the user sends to employees of a tracked company, and (5) a dashboard that visualizes the full funnel from companies identified to applications submitted to replies received to interviews scheduled to offers extended.
The application is built on a Python 3.11 + Flask + SQLite stack running entirely on the user's local machine. No data leaves the device. LinkedIn scraping is rate-limited and operates within a real logged-in browser session to minimize ban risk. The MVP is scoped to ship in eight weeks across three phases: company upload and manual tracking (Weeks 1-3), LinkedIn hybrid sync and job detection (Weeks 4-6), and HR email logging, follow-ups, and analytics (Weeks 7-8). After MVP, optional Phase 4 will explore a browser extension, AI-assisted cold-email drafting, and calendar integration.
2. Problem Statement & Motivation
The current job-seeking workflow suffers from five concrete pain points that this product is designed to eliminate. Each pain point below is drawn from real, repeated friction observed during an active job search across the Kochi software company ecosystem (302 companies, sourced from Infopark official profiles).
2.1 Pain Point: Data Fragmentation Across Channels
Company shortlists live in an Excel spreadsheet with 19 columns of metadata (LinkedIn URL, career page, HR email, tech stack, match score). LinkedIn activity (connection requests, messages, profile views) lives inside the LinkedIn product UI and is not exportable. HR cold-emails sent from Gmail have no structured record. Follow-up dates exist only in the seeker's head or in scattered calendar reminders. There is no single source of truth that answers the question: "For company X, what have I done so far and what should I do next?"
2.2 Pain Point: Missed Follow-Ups
Industry data suggests that 60-70% of cold HR emails receive a response only after a follow-up. Without a structured reminder system tied to each outreach event, follow-ups are forgotten. The current spreadsheet has no native concept of follow-up cadence or due-date tracking.
2.3 Pain Point: No Visibility Into LinkedIn Funnel
LinkedIn does not expose a per-company view of "connection requests I sent to employees of company X" or "messages I exchanged with company X employees." The seeker must manually scroll through their LinkedIn My Network page and match names to companies, which is infeasible at 302-company scale.
2.4 Pain Point: Job Opening Detection Is Manual
Each of the 302 companies has a career page URL and a LinkedIn company page. Checking these manually for new openings is a daily chore that consumes 30-60 minutes. There is no automated delta detection ("new job posted since last check").
2.5 Pain Point: No Funnel Analytics
Without consolidated data, the seeker cannot answer strategic questions: What is my response rate? Which industries are converting best? Am I spending too much time on low-match-score companies? A dashboard with funnel visualization and basic cohort analysis is required.
The motivation for this product is to replace five disconnected tools (spreadsheet, LinkedIn, Gmail, calendar, memory) with one local-first dashboard that automates data collection, centralizes tracking, and surfaces actionable insights. The product is explicitly personal-use-only and runs on localhost to keep the data private and the LinkedIn scraping within ethical bounds.

4.1 Primary Persona: Adithya - Active Job Seeker
Adithya is a software developer in Kochi, Kerala, with 2-4 years of experience, actively seeking a new role. He maintains a curated spreadsheet of 302 Kochi software companies with rich metadata (LinkedIn URLs, career pages, HR emails, tech stack, match scores). He is comfortable with Python and basic web development, and he wants a tool that respects his data privacy by keeping everything local.
His daily job-seeking workflow involves: opening the spreadsheet to pick 5-10 target companies, visiting each company's LinkedIn page to identify employees to connect with, sending connection requests with personalized notes, checking the company career page for open roles, drafting and sending HR cold-emails, and (in theory) following up after 5-7 days. Technical context: Adithya runs macOS or Windows, is comfortable installing Python via venv, and has a LinkedIn Premium account. He is willing to authenticate LinkedIn inside the app once and accept that the app will use his session to scrape data. He values a clean, fast UI over feature richness.

6. Functional Requirements
This section specifies the seven core functional areas of the MVP. Each subsection covers feature description, inputs and outputs, acceptance criteria, and notable edge cases. The level of detail is sufficient for a solo developer (the builder) to implement without further design discussion.
6.1 Company Upload & Management
The application must accept a .xlsx file upload and parse it into the companies table. The expected schema is the 19-column Kochi companies format (see Appendix A for full column mapping). The parser must handle missing values gracefully ("Unknown" cells in the source spreadsheet must be preserved as NULL in the database, not as the literal string "Unknown").
Re-uploads must deduplicate by Company Name (case-insensitive trim). When a duplicate is detected, the user is prompted to either skip the duplicate, overwrite the existing record, or merge (prefer non-empty fields from either source). A bulk delete and per-row edit must be available. The companies list view must support filter-by (industry, match score range, hiring status, contact status) and sort-by (match score desc, name asc, last activity desc).
Acceptance criteria: (1) Uploading the 302-row kochi_software_companies.xlsx imports all rows in under 5 seconds. (2) Re-uploading the same file produces 0 duplicates and 0 data loss. (3) Filtering by industry = "AI / Machine Learning" returns the correct subset. (4) Editing a company's HR email persists across app restarts.
6.2 LinkedIn Authentication (Hybrid Approach)
The application uses a hybrid LinkedIn integration. The official LinkedIn OAuth2 API is used to authenticate the user's identity and obtain a long-lived access token (scopes: r_liteprofile, r_emailaddress). This satisfies the "login with LinkedIn" requirement and provides a verified identity. However, the official API does not expose the data the application needs (sent connection requests, message history, per-company employee lists). For those, the application uses Playwright to drive a real Chromium browser session using the user's saved login cookies.
Authentication flow: User clicks "Connect LinkedIn." The app launches a Playwright-controlled Chromium window pointing to linkedin.com/login. The user logs in normally, including 2FA if enabled. The app detects successful login (URL changes to linkedin.com/feed/) and saves the session cookies to an encrypted file (.sessions/linkedin.enc) using a key derived from a user-provided passphrase. On subsequent launches, the app restores the session. If LinkedIn challenges the session (CAPTCHA, re-login required), the app pauses and prompts the user to re-authenticate.
Acceptance criteria: (1) First-time auth flow completes in under 2 minutes. (2) Saved session survives app restart. (3) Session expiry is detected and user is re-prompted. (4) No LinkedIn credentials are stored in plaintext anywhere on disk.
6.3 Job Opening Detection
The application detects new job openings from two sources per company: (a) the company's career page URL (column 4 in the spreadsheet) and (b) the LinkedIn company jobs tab (constructed from the LinkedIn Company Page URL, column 3, by appending /jobs/).
For career page scraping, the app uses httpx + BeautifulSoup4 with company-specific parsers (a generic parser falls back to scanning for keywords like "careers," "openings," "apply," "job," with title extraction from common ATS patterns: Greenhouse, Lever, Workday, Ashby, BambooHR). For LinkedIn jobs, the app uses the authenticated Playwright session to visit the company's /jobs/ page and extract job titles, locations, and posting dates.
Detection runs on a configurable schedule (default: every 24 hours per company, staggered to avoid bursts). New openings are inserted into the openings table with a delta flag and surfaced in the dashboard as cards. Existing openings that disappear are marked as closed.
Acceptance criteria: (1) For 10 sample companies, the scraper correctly extracts at least 80% of visible openings. (2) New openings appear in the dashboard within 24 hours of being posted. (3) Closed openings are flagged within 48 hours. (4) The scraper respects robots.txt and rate limits (max 1 request per 5 seconds per domain).
6.4 HR Email Tracking
The application does not send emails. It logs emails the user has sent externally (typically via Gmail) and tracks the follow-up lifecycle. Each HR email log entry captures: company_id, recipient_email, recipient_name (optional), subject, body_excerpt (first 200 chars), sent_at, and a status (sent / replied / no-reply / bounced).
When the user logs an email, the app auto-suggests a follow-up date based on a configurable cadence (default: 5 business days after sent_at). The follow-up is created in the follow_ups table with status "pending" and due_on = suggested date. When the user marks the original email as "replied," any pending follow-ups for that email are auto-cancelled. When the user marks an email as "no-reply after follow-up," the company's contact status is updated to "cold."
Acceptance criteria: (1) Logging an HR email takes under 30 seconds end-to-end. (2) Follow-up date auto-calculation excludes weekends. (3) Marking an email as "replied" cancels pending follow-ups. (4) The HR email appears in the company timeline immediately after logging.
6.5 LinkedIn Activity Tracking
This is the most technically ambitious feature. The application scrapes the user's own LinkedIn activity and maps it to tracked companies. Three event types are captured: (a) connection requests sent, (b) messages exchanged with connections who work at a tracked company, (c) profile views received from tracked-company employees (LinkedIn Premium feature).
Sync flow: A background job (triggered manually or on schedule) opens Playwright, navigates to linkedin.com/mynetwork/invitations/sent, scrolls to load all sent invitations (capped at 200 most recent), extracts recipient name, headline, and company. For each recipient, the app matches the company name against the companies table (fuzzy match with manual confirmation for ambiguous cases). Matched events are inserted into linkedin_events with type = "connection_sent." A second pass visits linkedin.com/messaging/ and for each thread where the recipient's company matches a tracked company, extracts the message thread (capped at 50 most recent threads).
Rate limiting: All LinkedIn scraping uses a randomized 8-15 second delay between page loads. The sync runs at most once every 6 hours. If LinkedIn returns a 999 status (rate limit) or CAPTCHA challenge, the sync aborts and alerts the user.
Acceptance criteria: (1) A manual sync completes within 5 minutes for a fresh account. (2) Connection requests sent in the last 7 days are captured with 95% accuracy. (3) Company matching has a 90%+ automatic match rate; ambiguous cases are queued for manual confirmation. (4) No more than 30 LinkedIn page loads per sync.
6.6 Follow-Up & Reminders
The follow-up system is the proactive layer that prevents missed outreach. Follow-ups are created in three ways: (a) automatically when an HR email is logged (default +5 business days), (b) manually by the user from the company detail page ("Schedule follow-up" with custom date and notes), (c) automatically when a LinkedIn connection is accepted but no message has been exchanged in 7 days.
Each follow-up has a status: pending, done, snoozed (with snooze_until date), or cancelled. The dashboard surfaces follow-ups due today, due this week, and overdue. A daily digest (configurable: email, in-app notification, or both) lists the day's required actions. Reminders respect a quiet-hours window (default: 9 PM - 8 AM local time) for in-app notifications.
Acceptance criteria: (1) Follow-ups due today appear in the dashboard banner. (2) Snoozing a follow-up moves it to the snoozed list and re-surfaces on snooze_until. (3) The daily digest is generated at the configured time. (4) Overdue follow-ups are visually highlighted.
6.7 Dashboard & Analytics
The dashboard is the home screen and the primary value proposition. It is organized into four zones: (1) Funnel chart (companies -> contacted -> replied -> interviewed -> offered) rendered with Chart.js as a horizontal funnel; (2) Today's actions (follow-ups due, new job openings detected, pending sync); (3) Recent activity timeline (last 20 events across all companies); (4) Quick filters (industry, match score range, last activity date).
The analytics tab provides cohort views: response rate by industry, response rate by company size, average days-to-reply, follow-up effectiveness (reply rate after 0/1/2 follow-ups). All charts are computed client-side from JSON endpoints to keep the server stateless and fast.
Acceptance criteria: (1) Dashboard loads in under 2 seconds with 302 companies. (2) Funnel chart updates immediately when an email is logged or a status changes. (3) All analytics are computed from real data; no placeholders. (4) The dashboard is the default landing page after login.

8.8 Entity Relationship Summary
The relationships are: companies (1) to (N) applications; companies (1) to (N) contacts; companies (1) to (N) openings; companies (1) to (N) linkedin_events; contacts (1) to (N) linkedin_events; applications (1) to (N) follow_ups; contacts (1) to (N) follow_ups; companies (1) to (N) follow_ups; openings (1) to (1) applications (when a user applies to a specific opening). All foreign keys have ON DELETE CASCADE except where explicitly noted, so deleting a company removes all related child rows. The companies table is the central hub; every other table references it directly or transitively.

9.2 Architecture Overview
The application follows a classic three-tier monolith: Presentation (Jinja2 templates + Bootstrap + Chart.js), Application (Flask blueprints), and Data (SQLite via SQLAlchemy). There is no separate backend API server; the Flask app serves both HTML pages and JSON endpoints (under /api/) from the same process. 9.3 Data Flows
9.3.1 Spreadsheet Upload Flow
User POSTs .xlsx to /companies/upload -> openpyxl parses to list of dicts -> validation (required fields, deduplication) -> SQLAlchemy bulk insert into companies -> redirect to /companies with success flash. The whole flow is synchronous because 302 rows takes < 5 seconds.
9.3.2 LinkedIn Sync Flow
User clicks "Sync now" on /linkedin -> POST /linkedin/sync -> APScheduler spawns background job -> job loads encrypted session -> Playwright opens Chromium -> navigates through My Network sent invitations -> extracts recipients -> matching service maps to companies -> inserts into linkedin_events -> job updates scraping_jobs row with counts -> dashboard auto-refreshes via polling /api/sync-status.
9.3.3 Job Opening Detection Flow
APScheduler fires every 24h (staggered per company) -> selects 20 companies due for scan -> for each: if career_page_url present, httpx GET + BeautifulSoup parse -> if linkedin_url present, Playwright GET to /jobs/ -> normalize results -> compare against openings table -> insert new opening rows -> mark disappeared openings as closed -> update scraping_jobs.

14. Acceptance Criteria
Each criterion below is testable and ties to a specific functional requirement. All must pass for the MVP to be considered complete. Criteria are grouped by feature area.
14.1 Company Upload & Management
Given the kochi_software_companies.xlsx file, when the user uploads it, then all 302 rows are inserted into companies table within 5 seconds.
Given the spreadsheet is already uploaded, when the user re-uploads the same file, then 0 duplicate rows are created and 0 rows are lost.
Given 302 companies in the database, when the user filters by industry = 'AI / Machine Learning', then the result count matches the spreadsheet's filter on the same column (within +/- 2 rows for boundary cases).
Given a company with hr_email = 'careers@example.com', when the user edits it to 'hr@example.com' and refreshes, then the new value persists.
14.2 LinkedIn Authentication
Given a fresh install, when the user clicks 'Connect LinkedIn' and completes OAuth2 + session login, then the dashboard shows 'LinkedIn: Connected as <name>' within 2 minutes.
Given a connected session, when the user closes and reopens the app, then the session is still valid and no re-login is required.
Given LinkedIn session has expired, when the user clicks 'Sync Now', then the app detects the redirect to /login, aborts the sync, and prompts the user to re-authenticate.
14.3 LinkedIn Sync
Given the user has sent 20 LinkedIn connection requests in the last 7 days to employees of tracked companies, when a sync runs, then at least 18 of those 20 are captured in linkedin_events with type = 'connection_sent' and correctly mapped to companies.
Given a sync is in progress, when the user navigates to /linkedin, then they see a live progress indicator showing items_processed count updating.
Given LinkedIn returns a 999 status, when the sync retries with backoff and still fails, then the sync is aborted with status = 'aborted' and the user sees a yellow banner on the dashboard.
14.4 Job Opening Detection
Given a company with an open career page listing 5 jobs, when the scraper runs, then at least 4 of those 5 are inserted into openings with status = 'open'.
Given an opening that was open yesterday and is now closed, when the next scrape runs, then the opening's status is updated to 'closed' within 48 hours.
Given a career page that is a JavaScript SPA, when httpx fails to find job listings, then the scraper falls back to Playwright rendering and succeeds.
14.5 HR Email & Follow-Up Tracking
Given a company detail page is open, when the user clicks 'Log Email' and submits the modal, then the email appears in the company timeline within 1 second.
Given an HR email was logged with sent_at = today, when the follow-up is auto-created, then due_on is set to 5 business days from today (excluding weekends).
Given a follow-up is due today, when the user opens the dashboard, then the follow-up appears in the 'Today's Actions' card.
Given a follow-up is due today, when the user marks it 'done', then it disappears from 'Today's Actions' and moves to a 'Completed Today' list.
14.6 Dashboard & Analytics
Given 302 companies with varied contact_status, when the user opens the dashboard, then the funnel chart renders in under 2 seconds with correct counts at each stage.
Given the user logs a new HR email, when they refresh the dashboard, then the 'Contacted' count in the funnel chart increments by 1.
Given the user navigates to the analytics tab, when they view the 'Response rate by industry' chart, then each industry's response rate is computed as (replied count) / (emailed count) for that industry.
14.7 Export & Backup
Given any state of the database, when the user clicks 'Export CSV', then a file downloads containing all companies with their latest contact_status and last_activity_at.
Given the app has been running for 30+ days, when the user checks ~/.jobtracker/backups/, then there are 30 daily backup files and the oldest is no more than 30 days old.

Edge Case | Mitigation
LinkedIn session expired mid-sync | Detect redirect to /login, abort sync, mark scraping_job as 'aborted', alert user to re-authenticate, preserve all data captured before abort
LinkedIn returns 999 status (rate limit) | Exponential backoff: wait 60s, retry once. If still 999, abort sync and resume from last successful company on next scheduled run
LinkedIn CAPTCHA challenge appears | Pause Playwright, surface a notification to user, wait up to 10 minutes for manual solve. If user solves, continue; if timeout, abort gracefully
Company career page unreachable (404, DNS fail, timeout) | Log error to scraping_jobs.error_message, increment failure count, skip to next company. After 3 consecutive failures, mark company.career_page_url as 'broken' and exclude from future scans until user fixes the URL
xlsx column mismatch (missing required column 'Company Name') | Reject upload with specific error message listing missing columns. Suggest downloading the template format
Duplicate company on re-upload (same name, different case) | Case-insensitive trim comparison. Prompt user: skip / overwrite / merge. Default: skip with count of skipped duplicates
Company name fuzzy match ambiguous (e.g., 'TCS' vs 'Tata Consultancy Services') | Queue for manual confirmation in a 'Review Matches' UI. Do not auto-link ambiguous matches
Scraped job opening URL already exists | UNIQUE constraint on (company_id, title, url) catches it. Update last_seen_at instead of inserting duplicate
User marks HR email as 'replied' but pending follow-up exists | Auto-cancel all pending follow-ups for that email's company+contact. Notify user with toast: 'Cancelled 1 pending follow-up'
Follow-up due_on is in the past (e.g., user manually scheduled yesterday) | Accept and immediately mark as 'overdue' in dashboard banner. Do not auto-reschedule
User deletes a company that has 50+ child rows (applications, events, follow-ups) | Confirm modal showing child row counts. On confirm, CASCADE delete. Log to an audit_trail table for 30-day undo (future enhancement)
LinkedIn returns empty sent-invitations list (account flagged or new account) | Treat as successful sync with 0 new items. Do not error. Log a warning if this persists across 3 syncs
Career page is a JavaScript SPA (no static HTML) | Fall back to Playwright rendering for that domain. Cache the SPA-rendering flag per domain to avoid retrying with httpx
Spreadsheet has extra unknown columns | Ignore unknown columns; do not fail the import. Surface a warning listing ignored columns
App launched while another instance is already running | Bind lock file at ~/.jobtracker/.lock. On conflict, surface 'App already running' message and exit
